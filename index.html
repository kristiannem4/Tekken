Yoshimitsu

Yoshimitsu" isn't just one person. It is a title

passed down to the leader of the Manji Clan.

The Yoshimitsu in SoulCalibur (set in the 16th century) is

a distant ancestor of the one in Tekken.

He wields a demonic sword (also named Yoshimitsu) that hungers for souls.

To keep the sword from consuming his own mind,

he must use it to slay only those who are truly evil.

Hwoarang

Hwoarang is the "bad boy" of the Tekken series,

known for his flashy kicks, arrogance, and intense

rivalry with Jin Kazama. His entire motivation is usually to find and defeat

Jin Kazama. They fought to a draw before the events of Tekken 3,

and Hwoarang has been obsessed with a rematch ever since. He is a

master of Taekwondo, trained by Baek Doo San. Unlike most characters

who use their fists, Hwoarang’s moveset is almost entirely focused

on complex, high-pressure kick strings.

Nina Williams

Nina Williams is the deadliest assassin in the

Tekken universe, known for her cold personality,

blonde hair, and lethal fighting style.

Unlike most fighters who enter tournaments for glory,

Nina is a mercenary. She has been hired by various

organizations (including the Mishima Zaibatsu)

to carry out high-profile assassinations.Nina is technically much older than she looks.

Between Tekken 2 and 3, she (and Anna) were placed in Cryosleep

(Cold Sleep) for 19 years as part of an experiment.

This explains why she still looks young despite having a grown son.

Ling Xiaoyu

Ling Xiaoyu is the energetic "High School Girl"

of the Tekken series, known for her incredible agility,

cheerful personality, and her massive pet panda.

Her fighting style is based on Baguazhang and Piguaquan.

She is famous for her "Art of Phoenix" (AOP) stance,

where she crouches so low to the ground that many high attacks

simply fly over her head. Xiaoyu doesn't travel alone.

She is always accompanied by Panda, who is both her best friend

and a playable character in her own right.

Panda was trained by Heihachi Mishima specifically to protect Xiaoyu.

Eddy Gordo

Eddy introduced the world to the Brazilian martial art of Capoeira

in Tekken 3. His style combines acrobatics, dance, and music,

focusing on fluid movement and powerful leg sweeps.

Eddy wasn't always a fighter; he was a wealthy young man whose life

was ruined when his father was killed by a drug cartel.

He took the blame for the murder to protect himself in prison,

where he met an old master who taught him Capoeira.

Much of his story involves trying to find a cure for his master’s terminal

illness. This led him to serve Jin Kazama and the Tekken Force in exchange

for Mishima Zaibatsu’s medical technology.

Anna Williams

Anna Williams is the glamorous, treacherous, and highly competitive

younger sister of Nina Williams. While Nina is the "Silent Assassin,"

Anna is the "Scarlet Assassin"—louder, more stylish, and much more

provocative.Unlike Nina’s professional stoicism, Anna uses her charm

and beauty as a distraction. She often wears high-fashion silk dresses

(usually red) and strikes model-like poses even mid-fight to taunt

her opponents. While Nina often worked for Jin, Anna frequently aligned

herself with Kazuya Mishima and G Corporation.

She served as his personal bodyguard and

a high-ranking officer in his private army.

Armorking

Armor King is the dark, mysterious counterpart to King.

He is a professional wrestler who hides his identity behind a black,

one-eyed jaguar mask and heavy plate armor. Just like Yoshimitsu,

the title has been held by two people. The original Armor King (the mentor

of King I) was accidentally killed in a bar fight by Craig Marduk.

The current Armor King is the original’s younger brother,

who entered the tournament specifically to get revenge on Marduk.

While King uses "clean" Lucha Libre and power wrestling,

Armor King uses "Shoot Wrestling" and street fighting.

He is much more aggressive, using illegal moves, strikes, and dark energy

(like his green mist) to blind opponents.

King

King is the legendary Luchador of the Tekken series, famous for his iconic

jaguar mask and for communicating only through jaguar growls.

Just like Armor King, the title has been held by two different men.

The original King (from Tekken 1 and 2) was an orphan-turned-priest

who fought to fund his orphanage.

The current King was one of those orphans who took up the mask to honor

his mentor's memory after he was killed by Ogre. Unlike the dark and

vengeful Armor King, King is a pure hero. He fights for his orphanage,

helps his community, and even eventually forgave Craig Marduk

(the man who killed his mentor), choosing peace over revenge.

Jin Kazama

Jin Kazama is the main protagonist of the Tekken series and

one of its most complex characters.

He is defined by his tragic lineage, his "Devil Gene," and his quest to end

the Mishima bloodline. Introduced in Tekken 3, Jin is the son of

Kazuya Mishima and Jun Kazama.

He was raised by his mother with peaceful pacifist values until

she was attacked by Ogre, forcing Jin to seek out his grandfather,

Heihachi Mishima, for training. In Tekken 3, Jin fought using

"Mishima StyleKarate"(like his father). However, to distance himself

from his family, he "unlearned" that style and switched to Traditional

Karate from Tekken 4 onwards.

Jun Kazama

Jun Kazama is the moral compass of the Tekken series and the mother of

Jin Kazama. She represents light and purity in a world dominated by the

"Devil Gene."After her debut in Tekken 2, Jun disappeared for nearly

30 years in real-world time. She was presumed dead after being

attacked by Ogre, but Tekken 8 finally revealed she had been recovering

in a mystical sanctuary.

Unlike the Mishimas who use lightning, Jun’s attacks in Tekken 8

are infused with white light and flower petals.

She can even heal herself during a fight by

using her special "Life-Force" moves.

Heihachi Mishima

Heihachi Mishima is the original "King of Iron Fist" and the patriarch of the

most dysfunctional family in gaming history. He is the ultimate

"love-to-hate" villain.Heihachi is the long-time head of the

Mishima Zaibatsu. He is known for his "survival of the fittest"

mentality, which led him to throw his young son, Kazuya, off a cliff

to see if he was strong enough to survive. Unlike his son Kazuya

or grandson Jin, Heihachi does not have the Devil Gene.

He is a pure human who reached superhuman strength through sheer

willpower and brutal training in Mishima Style Fighting Karate.

Kunimitsu

Kunimitsu is the rival of Yoshimitsu and a former member of the

Manji Clan. She is a thief who was expelled for stealing from

the clan for personal gain, leading to a lifelong grudge.

Like Yoshimitsu, there are actually two Kunimitsus. The original (mother)

appeared in the early games, while her daughter (Kunimitsu II)

took over the mantle in Tekken 7. Both wear fox masks (Kitsune)

to hide their faces.Her primary goal is to steal Yoshimitsu's self-named

sword. She believes the blade belongs to her grandfather and has spent

years hunting Yoshimitsu to take it back.

Paul Phoenix

Paul Phoenix is the hot-headed, American biker who has appeared in every

single Tekken game. He is the definition of "raw power"

and is famous for having the tallest hair in fighting game history.

In the early games, Paul was a serious contender

and the primary rival to Kazuya Mishima. He is actually one

of the few humans strong enough to have fought Kazuya to a draw.

While he started as a serious martial artist, his storyline drifted into

comedy. He is often portrayed as being perpetually broke, unlucky, and

obsessed with proving he is the strongest—even

if it means picking fights with aliens or bears.

Marshall Law

Marshall Law is the "Legendary Dragon" of the Tekken series and a direct,

loving tribute to the martial arts icon Bruce Lee.

From his high-pitched battle cries ("Wataaa!") to his yellow tracksuit

and his fighting style, Jeet Kune Do, Law is a mirror of Bruce Lee.

He uses fast punches, backflips, and his signature one-inch punch.

Unlike the billionaire Mishimas, Law is a working-class hero.

He usually enters tournaments to pay off massive debts,

fund his martial arts dojo, or save his failing Chinese restaurant.

Lei Wulong

Lei Wulong is the "Super Cop" of the Tekken series and a direct

tribute to the action movie legend Jackie Chan.

He is widely considered the most complex and creative character

in the entire franchise. Lei is a Hong Kong police officer known

for his clumsybut-brilliant fighting style.

Just like Jackie Chan's characters, he is often funny,

a bit disorganized, but incredibly skilled.

He even has a "Drunken Master" style.

Lei is famous for having the most stances in the game.

He can switch between five animal styles (Tiger, Crane, Leopard, Snake,

and Dragon), as well as his Drunken style.

Bryan Fury

Originally a ruthless kickboxer and an officer in Interpol,

Bryan was killed during a shootout in Hong Kong. He was reanimated

as a zombie cyborg by the scientist Dr. Abel. Later,

he was fitted with a perpetual energy generator by Dr. Bosconovitch,

granting him limitless stamina.

Bryan is defined by his sadistic nature, often accompanied by his

trademark maniacal laugh during combat. He has no moral

compass and lives only for chaos and destruction.

is the arch-enemy of Yoshimitsu, the leader of the Manji Clan,

after Bryan repaid Dr. Bosconovitch's life-saving repairs by attacking him.

Kazuya Mishima

At age five, his father, Heihachi Mishima, threw him off a cliff to test his

strength and "weakness." Kazuya survived only by making a pact with

the Devil Gene, a supernatural power inherited from his

mother, Kazumi. Kazuya is depicted as a cold, power-hungry oppressor.

His name ironically means "peaceful one,"

contrasting sharply with his misanthropic and arrogant nature.

In Tekken 8, he leads the G Corporation in a brutal

war for world domination,

serving as the primary rival to his own son, Jin Kazama.

Bruce Irvin

Bruce Irvin is a major supporting character in the Tekken series, first

appearing in Tekken 2 as a loyal bodyguard to Kazuya Mishima. He is

widely recognized for his powerful Muay Thai fighting style.

After surviving a plane crash arranged by Kazuya's men

(which left him with amnesia), he became Kazuya's right-hand man.

Unlike many of Kazuya's subordinates,

Bruce respects him and they share a mutual friendship rooted in their

similarly rough upbringings.Bruce was last playable

in Tekken 6 as the head of G Corporation's private army.

He has been absent from the base rosters of Tekken 7 and Tekken 8.

Kuma

Kuma is the name of two different characters in the Tekken series, both

of whom are literal brown bears who serve as the loyal

bodyguards and pets of Heihachi Mishima. The original

Kuma appeared in Tekken 1 and 2. After he died of old age, his son, Kuma II,

took his place starting in Tekken 3 and has appeared in every game since.

Unlike normal bears, Kuma is highly intelligent. He was trained by Heihachi

in the Mishima style of martial arts and can understand human

speech, though he only "speaks" in growls.

Jack

Jack (often called Jack-1) is the original mass-produced military

android in the Tekken series. Developed by the Soviet Union,

he was sent to the first King of Iron Fist Tournament to stop

Kazuya Mishima’s coup of the Mishima Zaibatsu. Jack-1 is the

"father" of the entire Jack series (Jack-2, Gun Jack, Jack-5, Jack-6, Jack-7,

and Jack-8). Every subsequent Jack is an upgraded

version of this original chassis. His primary objective in Tekken 1 was to

eliminate Kazuya Mishima. However, he failed his mission and

was eventually succeeded by the more advanced, "sentient" Jack-2.

Lee Chaolan

Lee Chaolan is one of the most stylish characters in Tekken,

known for his catchphrase "Excellent!" and his silver hair.

He first appeared in the original Tekken as the adopted son of

Heihachi Mishima. Heihachi adopted Lee specifically to be a rival for

Kazuya. This created a lifelong hatred between the brothers.

After being expelled from the Mishima family, Lee became an

incredibly successful businessman. He founded Violet Systems,

a high-tech robotics company, and often fights

under the flamboyant alter-ego Violet.

Baek Doo San

Baek Doo San is the first true master of Taekwondo in the Tekken series,

debuting in Tekken 2. He is a strict, disciplined

teacher known for his lightning-fast kicks and his relationship with his

student, Hwoarang. Baek is a world-class

Taekwondo champion who opened his own dojo. He is much

more serious and traditional than his hot-headed student,

Hwoarang. He accidentally killed his father during a training session

when he was young. This trauma fueled his drive for

perfection but also gave him a deep-seated guilt.

Michelle Chang

While Michelle is known for being a woman of action rather than words,

she has a very expressive personality when it comes

to her family and her cause. She isn't a "chatterbox," but her

dialogue and interactions show a woman

with a fiery spirit and a deep sense of justice.

Michelle’s story centers on a mysterious ancient

pendant passed down through her family.

Legend said it held the power to control

great spirits (or, according to some rumors, the "Ogre" entity).

Julia Chang

Julia Chang is the adopted daughter of Michelle Chang and debuted in

Tekken 3. She is a brilliant scientist and archaeologist who fights

to protect nature and her ancestral lands.

Initially, Julia entered the tournament to save her kidnapped mother.

Later, her goal shifted to reforestation—using her data

and research to restore the deserts of Arizona to lush forests.

In Tekken Tag Tournament 2, she temporarily fought

as a masked lucha libre wrestler named Jaycee to fill in for a friend.

Gun-Jack

Gun Jack is the third iteration of the Jack series, debuting in Tekken 3.

He was built by Jane, the little girl that Jack-2 rescued from a war zone in

Tekken 2. After Jack-2 was destroyed by a satellite laser, Jane

(now a brilliant scientist) recovered his memory data.

She built Gun Jack to test his combat capabilities

and retrieve the original Jack-2's core memories from the Mishima

Zaibatsu. Unlike the first Jack, Gun Jack has a soul.

His primary drive is to protect Jane, making him a "gentle giant"

when he isn't in combat.

Ganryu

Ganryu was a gifted Sumo wrestler who was banned from the sport for his

bad behavior, like gambling and fire-breathing.

He then became a high-ranking bodyguard for Kazuya Mishima.

He is famous (and infamous) for being obsessed with Michelle Chang,

and later her daughter Julia Chang. Most of his endings involve

him unsuccessfully trying to propose to them or "stalking" them in a

comedic way. While he can be greedy and a bit of a creep, Ganryu isn't

truly evil. In later games, he tries to be a "hero of justice" to impress Julia,

even becoming a sumo-themed streamer (Ganny-chan) in Tekken 7.

Dr. Bosconovitch

He is the scientific genius responsible for many of the series' icons.

He created Alisa Bosconovitch (modeled after his deceased daughter),

the Jack robots, and the bio-weapon Alex.

He is the one who saved Bryan Fury by giving him a mechanical body

and later a perpetual energy generator.

He also helped Yoshimitsu by giving him a prosthetic arm.

His primary goal throughout the series has been to find a way to

resurrect his daughter, Alisa. This often forces him to work for

dangerous people like the Mishimas.

Prototype Jack

Prototype Jack (or P. Jack) is the experimental first version of the

Jack series. He debuted in the first Tekken as a hidden sub-boss and

is the "rival" model to the standard Jack. He was the

first robot built by the Mishima Zaibatsu to test the feasibility of

a mechanical soldier. Unlike the "standard" Jack

(built by the Soviets), P. Jack was an unrefined prototype.

He has a built-in rivalry with Jack-2. In Tekken 2, he was

sent specifically to destroy Jack-2 and retrieve the data gathered

by his "brother."

Devil Kazuya

Devil Kazuya is the demonic transformation of Kazuya Mishima.

He represents Kazuya's soul after it was consumed by the Devil Gene,

a supernatural curse he used to survive being thrown

off a cliff by his father. While Kazuya and Devil

were originally seen as two entities fighting for control,

they eventually merged into one. Kazuya now has full control over his

demonic powers. He is the primary manifestation of the "Mishima Curse."

His goal is to eliminate his son, Jin Kazama,

to take Jin's half of the Devil Gene and become an all-powerful god.

Ogre

Ogre (known in Japan as Toshin, the "Fighting God") is the primary

antagonist of Tekken 3. He is an ancient, biological weapon left on Earth by

aliens in the distant past. Ogre was awakened from his sleep

in a Mexican temple by the Mishima Zaibatsu's excavations.

He is a "God of Fighting" who hunts down the world's

strongest martial artists to steal their souls and techniques.

According to the lore, Ogre attacked and "killed" (or caused the

disappearance of) several classic characters, including Jun Kazama,

Baek Doo San, and King I.

True Ogre

True Ogre is the final boss of Tekken 3 and the evolved, monstrous form

of the "Fighting God." He appears when Ancient Ogre absorbs

the soul of Heihachi Mishima (or Jinpachi) to regain his full

power. While Ancient Ogre looks like a warrior, True Ogre is a literal

monster. He is a massive, winged chimera that represents the peak of

destructive biological engineering. In the story of

Tekken 3, he is the ultimate obstacle for Jin Kazama, who seeks revenge

for the "death" of his mother, Jun.

Roger

Roger is the original genetically engineered boxing kangaroo in the Tekken

series. Like Alex the dinosaur, he was created by Dr. Bosconovitch

in the Mishima Zaibatsu biotech labs. Roger debuted in Tekken 2

as a "transgenic military animal" created under Kazuya Mishima's

corrupt leadership. Kazuya eventually deemed him a "useless failure".

After the events of Tekken 2, Roger lived in peace in Australia and

had a son, Roger Jr.. Years later, Roger was kidnapped, prompting

his wife and son to enter the King of Iron Fist Tournament 5 to find him.
